What is
Google Search?

Google Search (also known simply as Google) is a search engine provided by Google.

It is also the most-visited website in the world.

PageRank

The order of search results returned by Google is based, in part, on a priority rank system called "PageRank".

Google Search also provides many different options for customized searches, using symbols and offers specialized interactive experiences.

Search Syntax

Google search accepts queries as normal text, as well as individual keywords.

It automatically corrects misspelled words, and yields the same results regardless of capitalization.

For more customized results, one can use a wide variety of operators.

Operators

OR – Search for webpages containing one of two similar queries, such as marathon OR race

- (minus sign) – Exclude a word or a phrase, so that "apple -tree" searches where word "tree" is not used

"" – Force inclusion of a word or a phrase, such as "tallest building"

* – Placeholder symbol allowing for any substitute words in the context of the query, such as "largest * in the world"

.. – Search within a range of numbers, such as "camera $50..$100"

Some Commands

site: – Search within a specific website, such as "site:youtube.com"

define: – See a definition of a word, such as "define:phrase"

related: – Find webpages related to specific URL addresses, such as "related:www.wikipedia.org"

cache: – Highlights the search-words within the cached pages, so that "cache:www.google.com xxx" shows cached content with word "xxx" highlighted.

filetype: – Search for a single file type, such as "filetype:pdf JCVD"

allintitle:, allintext:, allinurl: – Concerns all the words that follow the command and allows you to search either in the title, the text or the url.

Other features

Depersonalized search results:

it is possible to neutralize the results. Just add the operator &pws=0 at the end of the search results URL. You can automate this action by adding a search engine in Google Chrome for example, with the address:

https://www.google.com/
search?q=%s&pws=0

Calculate and convert

Type a mathematical operation in the Google search bar to get the result.

It is possible to display the curve of a mathematical function by entering its definition.

Google can also convert units by typing queries such as 123 mph = km/h (conversion from miles per hour to km/h).
